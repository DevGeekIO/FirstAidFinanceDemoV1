Hannah, Cosmetic Nurse
Self-Employed / No Tax Returns
Approved @ Specialist Rate

Introduction

At First Aid Finance, we recently helped Hannah, a cosmetic nurse who was struggling to purchase a new vehicle for her business due to her recent switch to self-employment and lack of tax returns.

Despite being asset-backed and a professional health specialist, Hannah could not secure financing from other lenders. She also wanted to ensure she could access a competitive interest rate that reflected her profession as a nurse. We listened to her concerns and were confident we could find a solution that met her unique needs.

The Challenge

We recognised that self-employed individuals like Hannah often face obstacles when trying to secure financing. In Hannah’s case, her recent switch to self-employment and lack of tax returns presented further challenges.

Most traditional banks rely heavily on two years of tax returns to verify income. Without these, automated systems simply decline the application.

In addition to these hurdles, she was also looking for a specialised interest rate that reflected her profession as a nurse, ensuring she wasn't penalized with "Low Doc" higher rates.

"We were able to secure financing for her without requiring any additional documentation to prove her income or business assets."

The Solution

We took the time to understand Hannah’s unique situation and worked closely with her to identify a specialist lender who was willing to work with her despite her recent self-employment status and lack of tax returns. We also identified a specialised interest rate reflecting her nursing profession.

We carefully assessed Hannah’s financial position and demonstrated to the lender that her assets were sufficient to secure financing for her new vehicle. We leveraged our relationship with the lender to argue the strength of her industry experience over her current paperwork status.

Results

Thanks to our tailored solution, Hannah was able to purchase the new vehicle she needed to support her growing business.

  • Approval granted within 48 hours.
  • No tax returns required.
  • Interest rate matched standard full-doc products.

She was also able to access a specialised interest rate that reflected her profession as a nurse, lowering her costs even further.

Conclusion

At First Aid Finance, we understand the challenges that self-employed individuals face when trying to secure financing. We strive to provide customised solutions that meet the unique needs of each of our clients, even in situations where traditional lenders may not be willing to work with them.

We were proud to assist Hannah, a cosmetic nurse who faced obstacles in purchasing a new vehicle for her business due to her recent switch to self-employment and lack of tax returns. We encourage other self-employed individuals in similar situations to consider First Aid Finance as a lender of choice. Our expertise and experience can help you find a tailored solution that meets your needs.
